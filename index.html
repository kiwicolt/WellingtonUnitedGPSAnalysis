Introduction
Two GPS systems,
one complete picture

Wellington United Diamonds use two GPS tracking systems simultaneously: Catapult and StatSports. Because different players wear different devices, the two systems track entirely different groups of players within the same match — there is no overlap, and no reconciliation between systems for the same individual is required or possible.

This report consolidates both datasets into a single unified view per game, with consistent metrics presented for every player regardless of device. Where metrics differ in definition between systems — particularly acceleration, deceleration, and high-speed distance — this is noted clearly throughout and the figures should not be directly compared across systems.

This update extends the season report from six to twelve tracked fixtures, covering 17 May through 27 June 2026. One of the twelve — the 29 May fixture vs Phoenix — was a cup tie that went to extra time and penalties rather than a standard Women's Central League match; it is included with a clear marker throughout and a specific methodology note given its unusual length. Wellington United remain in a title race with Waterside Karori and Petone, with further WCL rounds still to play.

Data methodology
How the data
was processed

Raw GPS exports from both systems require processing to produce accurate, comparable per-game figures. GPS devices continue recording before and after match time — including warm-ups, half-time, and cool-downs — meaning raw session totals substantially overstate actual playing time and distance. The pipeline below converts raw exports into accurate match-time-only figures for every player in every game, and was applied consistently across all twelve fixtures.

Standard processing (10 of 12 games): Catapult data extracted from 1st.half and 2nd.half splits with the game tag only, excluding the all split which includes warm-up. StatSports data extracted from the Total session row, cross-checked against First Half/Second Half rows. Playing-time formula: a half counts as played if Distance ≥ 800m AND Distance/min ≥ 25m/min.

vs Miramar (23 May) — export issue identified and corrected: the Catapult half-split Duration field for this match was found to be bugged — it returned each player's total recording duration split evenly in two, rather than genuine first-half and second-half clock time. Distance per half remained accurate. The fix: standard half lengths (45.2 / 45.0 minutes) were derived from StatSports players in the same match (who had reliable First Half / Second Half data) and applied uniformly to all Catapult players, with the playing-time formula then run against the corrected duration and the genuine half-by-half distance.

vs Phoenix (29 May) — cup tie, extra time and penalties: this fixture is not a standard WCL match. Coach-confirmed structure: 90 minutes regulation plus 30 minutes extra time, followed by a penalty shootout. Playing time was assigned directly per coach confirmation rather than via the automated formula, since neither system's half-split data was usable for this match (Catapult had the same duration-bug as Miramar; StatSports recorded the match as a single undivided session). Marla MacInnis, Kennedy Corkin, Liv Catherwood, Georgie Furnell, Lara Smith, and Natalie Brook played the full 120 minutes; Jemma Robertson, Leo Webb, Rewa, and Molly Simons played the 90 minutes of regulation time before being substituted ahead of extra time; Jade Gawtrey's exact substitution time was not known and her ~44-minute figure is an estimate derived from teammates' distance-per-minute rate in the same situation, flagged accordingly. Because total recorded duration for this match included long non-playing buffer periods (warm-up, half-time, extra-time break, penalty shootout), distance and rate figures for this specific game should be treated as indicative rather than as precisely comparable to other fixtures.

Metric definitions: High Speed Distance — Catapult: Speed Zones 4+5 combined; StatSports: High Speed Running metric. High Accelerations/Decelerations — Catapult: efforts ≥2 m/s²; StatSports: all recorded events (different thresholds — not directly comparable across systems). Player Load is a Catapult-proprietary composite metric and is not available from StatSports.
