Introduction
Two GPS systems,
one complete picture

Wellington United Diamonds use two GPS tracking systems simultaneously: Catapult and StatSports. Because different players wear different devices, the two systems track entirely different groups of players within the same match — there is no overlap, and no reconciliation between systems for the same individual is required or possible.

This report consolidates both datasets into a single unified view per game, with consistent metrics presented for every player regardless of device. Where metrics differ in definition between systems — particularly acceleration, deceleration, and high-speed distance — this is noted clearly throughout and the figures should not be directly compared across systems.

Wellington United sit in a title race with Waterside Karori and Petone in the 2026 Women's Central League, a 10-team competition with many rounds remaining. The data in this report covers the first six games of the campaign.

Catapult  ·  worn by 12–15 players per game · provides Player Load and detailed speed zone data
StatSports  ·  worn by 2–6 players per game · provides High Speed Running and sprint event data
Data methodology
How the data
was processed

Raw GPS exports from both systems required significant processing to produce accurate, comparable per-game figures. The key challenge was that GPS devices continue recording before and after match time — including warm-ups, half-time, and cool-downs — meaning raw session totals substantially overstate actual playing time and distance.

The processing pipeline used below converts raw GPS exports into accurate match-time-only figures for every player in every game.

Catapult extraction: Data extracted from 1st.half and 2nd.half splits with the game tag only. The all split (which covers the full device recording window including warm-up) was excluded. Additive metrics summed across halves; peak metrics (top speed, max acceleration) taken as the maximum of the two halves.

StatSports extraction: Data extracted from the Total session row, with First Half and Second Half rows used to verify duration estimates. Duration calculated as Total Distance ÷ Distance per Minute (StatSports rounds distance per minute to the nearest integer, introducing minor rounding variation).

Playing-time formula: A half counts as "played" if Distance ≥ 800m AND Distance/min ≥ 25m/min. Both conditions must be met. This correctly identifies and excludes: device-on-bench readings (3–12m/min regardless of duration), goalkeeper warm-up tracking, and players who were not on the pitch. One manual override applied: Leo Webb vs VicUni 2nd half capped at 8 minutes on-pitch per coach confirmation.

Coach confirmations applied: Farina Anchico and Marla MacInnis vs Wairarapa came off early in the 2nd half (included as 1st-half-only but with 2nd-half distance retained). Carolyn O'Reilly vs Seatoun came on at half-time. Leo Webb vs VicUni came off at the 53-minute mark.

Metric definitions: High Speed Distance — Catapult: Speed Zones 4+5 combined; StatSports: High Speed Running metric. High Accelerations/Decelerations — Catapult: efforts ≥2 m/s²; StatSports: all recorded events (different thresholds — directionally useful within-system, not directly comparable across systems). Player Load is a Catapult-proprietary composite metric and is not available from StatSports.

Data accuracy statement: All figures in this report reflect GPS-recorded data only. The playing-time formula and coach confirmations have been applied to correct for device recording outside of match time. Where data appears anomalous — particularly very low distances or speeds consistent with goalkeeper warm-up rather than outfield play — this is flagged in the relevant player narrative. Coaches should use this data in conjunction with their own match observations.
